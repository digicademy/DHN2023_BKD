One Document does it all

Simplification & empowerment

BKD made good experiences with TEI customizing with ODD (one document does it all) for documentation as well as schema development. By now, the TEI-BKD ODD is maintained by the editors.

metadata managment

openRefine and Wikidata

person BKD-key GND | wikidata
place BKD-key geonames | wikidata
As far as possible, we link person data with GND (German Authority File), place data with geonames. Where this is not possible, Wikidata entries are created. There is hardly any standards data on organisation and events in particular. OpenRefine has proven to be an extremely helpful tool for indexing, cleaning, enriching and managing the extensive metadata.

lessons learned

One "lesson learned" of the student internship project, for example, was that it makes a lot of sense to include "implicit" entities, i.e. entities not explicitly mentioned in the correspondence ("the elephant in the room") explicitly in the metadata (=Type B).

proof of concept

... dare more hackathon ...

As part of the student internship project, the cooperation with relevant graph data bank experts and the hackathon "DH Jewish Studies", the relational data model was and is tested for resilience at a fairly early stage of the project - by external experts. These tests were not really planned, rather serendipity. The experience that third parties understand BKD data, BKD relations and the BKD data model in principle, and that from the and actually enable relevant analyses and visualisations from the abstract distinction of the relations is reassuring feedback. Positive was also that, if necessary, weaknesses in the model could be recognised early on and adjustments made.
